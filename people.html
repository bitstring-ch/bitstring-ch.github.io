Luigi Sgro

A Software Developer and Architect with a University Degree in Engineering, I started programming computers as a hobby when I was a teenager, and continued to do it professionally after the University.

I developed an early passion for C language, system programming and the UNIX operating system. I have been an enthusiastic Linux user and advocate since 1996.

In 2007 I decided to concentrate on Java, and soon after, I started working in the finance sector.

In 2010 I moved to London and continued my education with CQF (Certificate in Quantitative Finance), getting certified in July 2011, while working as software developer at Citigroup.

Thanks to the rapid technological evolution undergoing in the financial industry, starting with my first assignment in London, I had the option to work on several technologies, on a variety of challenging applications: parallel computation, memory caches, micro-services, severless architecture.

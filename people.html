Luigi Sgro

A software developer and architect, I have had the opportunity to start programming computers professionally right after the university.

My early passions were C language, system programming and the UNIX operating system. I have been an enthusiastic Linux user and advocate from 1996.

In 2007 I decided to concentrate on Java, and soon after, I started to work in the finance sector.

In 2010 I moved to London, to attend the course for the Certificate in Quantitative Finance, while working as developer for Citigroup. I passed all the exams and got certified in July 2011.

Thanks to the rapid technological evolution undergoing in the financial industry, starting with my first assignment in London, I had the option to work on several technologies, on a variety of challenging applications: parallel computation, memory caches, micro-services, severless architecture.

Since 2013 I transitioned from Java to the Scala language, maintaining a keen interest in the evolution of Java and its ecosystem.

Over the last three years, while working on pricing and risk systems, I acquired hands-on knowledge on a number of languages and frameworks popular in analytics: Python, R, Matlab, Spark.

From 2019 I work as free-lance in the Zurich/Zug region, looking for ways to leverage my knowledge and experience to provide high quality software and leading edge consulting to corporate clients.
